How To Invest in 2023: Top Trends

The year 2022 brought about a lot of change to almost everyone on the planet. The economy is already going through a recession, people see prices rising, and it seems like it's not the best time for investing. But this is not necessarily the case. Today, we will look at things from a different perspective and talk about a few trends of investing that could help you save and make money in the next 12 months.

In the year 2023, just like with any other year, we recommend you do extensive research before getting into the investing game. One rule of thumb that will help you to stay profitable is that you carefully consider which assets to get. The choice of assets in your portfolio should depend on the timing of investment and the budget you have available - make sure you invest the spare money, not the funds that you need for life. If you're looking for a short-term return, you might want to consider investing in deposits or bonds, while shares might be more effective in a more long-term perspective.

Now, let us consider a few options that might be popular amongst investors in 2023, as well as look into why they might prefer one over another.

  1. Bonds: quite a traditional approach to investing in bonds will continue to be popular in 2023. However, in the next year, it's best to choose them according to the degree of their reliability: For instance, in addition to federal loan bonds (OFZ), investors should also look at papers, the issue of which has a credit rating on the national scale BBB and higher. According to the recommendations of expert investors, it might be a good idea to make bonds equal to about 50% of your investment portfolio. That percentage allows you to competently and carefully allocate 6-8 diversified bonds with a maturity length of 2-3 years from the date of purchase, which will include both OFZ and higher-yielding bonds 1-2 echelon.
  2. Funds and index investing: for all those who prefer to trust their funds to organisations and reap the benefits - investment funds and index investing will still be among the effective instruments in 2023. There exists a huge selection of stocks and exchange-traded funds (ETFs) all over the world and you can choose the ones that fit your purposes the best. Experts say that the most promising sectors for investment in 2023 will be fintech, online trading, cloud services, and semiconductors, as all of these have experienced a significant fall in 2022. Therefore, there are likely opportunities for its recovery and rise in 2023.
  3. Dividends: this year is likely to be interesting in terms of dividend yield and payouts. For instance, by the end of last year, companies, mainly raw materials, approved dividend payments, and given the strong decline in share prices, the yield on them is double-digit. Thus, investments in securities that are predicted to grow can also bring dividend yields, thereby significantly outpacing inflation. One might want to pay attention to the securities of companies focused on the domestic market, such as retailers and banks, retail sectors, and technology companies.
  4. Gold: Just like other precious metals, gold is likely to perform well in the next 12 months. This is mainly due to the changes in the US rate cycle. Now, investors can diversify their portfolio with silver and platinum, as well as gold, as they are showing significant growth rates. However, experienced analysts recommend not allocating more than 10% to metals in your portfolio.

Above, we discussed just a few options on how to approach investments as a trader. However, the market is not limited to these and it's always best to do thorough research and find options that play well with your and your goals. The instruments described above relate only to the stock market and trading, but there are also other ways to invest.

One such way is investing in start-ups. In the next section, we are going to talk about a few ways you can do so, as well as consider the pros and cons of putting your money into a small or medium-sized business.

Investig in start-ups works quite similarly, regardless of what funding algorithm you choose to stick to. Usually, here's what happens: an investor provides the founders of a start-up with a set amount of funds, and receives a share in the business in return. They would also usually have a right to claim a part of the expected profit, as well as give business advice (as they become shareholders and are interested n business success). However, if a start-up ends up failing - an investor is unlikely to get any profit or dividends - that's the beauty of venture capital investment!

Here are a few ways you can invest in a start-up:

  • Crowdinvesting - also known as the joint financing of a project by numerous investors in exchange for shares or a percentage of the company's profits.
  • Investing through brokers - intermediaries between startup entrepreneurs and investors. In many ways, it resembles the scheme with stock exchanges.
  • Investing through a venture fund - in this case, the projects which to invest in are decided upon by the management of the fund.
  • Investment club with partner participation - risks, profit, and responsibility are distributed in proportion to the amount of money invested.
  • Private financing - can be thought of as an investment of personal savings in exchange for shares in a startup.

It is important to remember that not all of these options are suitable for a beginner investor and that you will need to do more research and consult sources other than this guide.

Thus, investing in 2023 can be as fun and profitable as in previous years. However, in order to benefit from it, you will likely need to spend enough time doing preliminary research and creating a diversified enough portfolio. This way, if one asset would not end up performing that well, you will still have alternative sources of income and opportunities for growth.
